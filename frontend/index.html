How This System Works

What is a Markov Chain?

Think of it like predicting the weather. Today's weather depends only on yesterday's weather, not on the entire weather history. That's a Markov chain! In our system, your financial health tomorrow depends mainly on your financial state today. We use this to predict whether you'll move to a better or worse financial state next month.

P(next state = j | current state = i) = Transition Probability
"What's the chance I move to state j from state i next month?"

Financial States: Where Are You?

EXCELLENT Savings Ratio over 70%
You're crushing it! Saving more than 70% of what you earn. Super healthy finances.
GOOD Savings Ratio 40 to 70%
Nice! You're balancing income and expenses well. This is comfortable territory.
RISKY Savings Ratio 10 to 40%
You're spending more than you'd like. Getting tighter, needs some attention.
DEFAULT Savings Ratio 10% or less
Ouch! You're spending almost everything you earn. This is the danger zone.

How We Calculate Your Savings

The savings ratio is simple: how much of your monthly paycheck do you actually keep? This one number tells us everything about your financial health.

Savings Ratio = (Your Income - What You Spent) / Your Income

Real Example:
You earn = ₹50,000/month
You spend = ₹20,000/month
You save = (50,000 - 20,000) / 50,000 = 0.60 = 60%
Result: You're in GOOD state

This ratio is calculated for every single month in your bank statement. It's like a monthly health check for your wallet!

The Transition Matrix: Predicting Your Future

The transition matrix is like a probability roadmap. If you're in a GOOD state right now, what are the odds you'll stay there next month, improve, or slip into RISKY territory? This matrix has all those odds.

If you're currently in GOOD state this month:
10% chance you jump to EXCELLENT
80% chance you stay in GOOD
8% chance you slip to RISKY
2% chance you go all the way to DEFAULT

This comes from analyzing your actual transaction history. We see all the state changes you've had and build probabilities based on real patterns. All rows always add up to 100%.

Your Credit Score: How We Calculate It

Your credit score is based on your financial state right now, but we adjust it down if we think there's a risk you might default next month. Think of it as a reality check.

Credit Score = Base Score - (Default Risk x 300)

Base Scores at each state:
EXCELLENT state = 800 points
GOOD state = 700 points
RISKY state = 550 points
DEFAULT state = 300 points

Example Calculation:
You're in GOOD state (base 700)
Chance of default = 5% (0.05)
Score = 700 - (0.05 x 300) = 685

Your Credit Limit: How Much Credit Can You Get?

We let you borrow based on your income and risk level. The more likely you are to default, the less we'll lend you. It's a simple risk management strategy.

Credit Limit = (Your Income x 0.3) x (1 - Default Risk)

Real Example:
Average monthly income = ₹50,000
Default probability = 5% (0.05)

Calculation:
50,000 x 0.3 = ₹15,000
1 - 0.05 = 0.95
₹15,000 x 0.95 = ₹14,250 approved limit

The higher your risk of default, the lower your approved limit gets. It's fair and logical.

Behind the Scenes: How We Process Your Data

Here's the step-by-step journey your PDF takes through our system. All of this happens in seconds!

Step 1: We extract all transactions from your PDF
Step 2: We parse the date, money in (income), and money out (expenses)
Step 3: We group everything by month
Step 4: For each month, we calculate your savings ratio
Step 5: We assign you a financial state based on that ratio
Step 6: We build a transition matrix from all your state changes
Step 7: We predict what state you'll likely be in next month
Step 8: We calculate your credit score and approved limit

What We Need From You (The Fine Print)

For this system to work properly, we need certain things from your PDF. The more data you give us, the better our predictions.

Minimum data: 2 or more months of history
File format: PDF with a transaction table
Required columns in the table:
• Date of transaction
• Debit or Debit(N) column (money you spent)
• Credit or Credit(N) column (money you earned)

With just 2 months, our predictions are okay. With 12 months, they're much better. It's like building a weather forecast with more past data.
